Half Life 2

( the REVIEW article written by A.E.Kiss )

Summary:

+ Great game-play
+ Gravity gun
+ Atmosphere
+ Art-direction
+ Good expansions
+ Orange Box

Similar to:

Bioshock
Halo: Combat Evolved
Call of Duty 4: Modern Warfare

General information:

PC, PS3, Xbox, Xbox 360
First Person Shooter
November 16, 2004
Valve Corporation
Recommended age: 18+
Length: 8-20 hours (including expansions)

Raw score: 100%

Half Life 2 is a first person shooter that was developed and published by Valve in 2004. The aim of the game is traverse various levels and environments, by combatting enemies, driving different vehicles and solving some environment-based puzzles. The combat is fast paced and diverse that has stood the test of time, especially the timeless sections of the game focusing on the gravity gun. All other elements of gameplay show also a high level of polish and all players can expect 8-12 hours of fun while going through the campaign.

The world of Half Life 2 is set in a dystopian future, where Earth is invaded by a superior alien race due to the events in the original Half Life. Most of the game takes place in an Eastern-European region with strong influences of Soviet architecture. The story itself is good, albeit a bit vague, with well-written and -acted characters across the board.

The art-direction of Half Life 2 is fantastic. Voice acting and sound effects are still recognizable even more than 10 years after release. The music is generally absent and when it plays it does add very minimal to the overall atmosphere. The graphics were one generation ahead upon release, and they hold up surprisingly well despite the obvious aging.

Half Life 2 is recommended to all those who are into shooters. To those who like action games. To those who like a good atmosphere. To those who want to be immersed. To those who like digital entertainment. To those who like art. Because Half Life 2 shows how diverse only a video game can be, making it one the quintessential experiences of the medium.

Half Life 2: Episode 1 and Episode 2 are the expansions that follow the events of Half Life 2. Despite Episode 2 being clearly the better one, both expansions are highly recommended. Some of the best scenarios and levels are found in these expansion packs, with the same fun game-play and same level of polish found in the original game.
